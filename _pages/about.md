About Me
======
I'm Trey, I was born and raised here in Ann Arbor Michigan. I graduated from U of M in 2021 where I studied Mathematics and Philosophy, and have been working at the Institute for Social Research for the past 4 years as a part of the Population Neurodevelopment and Genetics Program. In my free time I play and watch a lot of soccer, enjoy going to the movies and read a lot of non-fiction books, although I have a weakness for sci-fi and fantasy. 

My research experience began in undergrad, working at the Bigham lab for anthropological genomics, looking at DNA Methylation of the HG19 pathway. I then worked at the Survey Research Center as a research assistant for the Health and Retirement Study, and the Michigan Lab of Geometry working on Conway’s Game of Life.  After Undergrad, I began working at the Institute for Social Research’s Biospecimen Lab, handling samples for the Study of Adolescent Neurodevelopment (SAND) and the Michigan Twin Neurogenetics Study (MTwiNS). I was then hired by the Population Neurodevelopment and Genetics Program at SRC, as a bioinformatician continuing my work with DNA Methylation. The studies I now work on include the Health and Retirement Study (HRS), the Future of Families and Child-Wellbeing Study (FFCW), the Indiana Person to Person Study (P2P), SAND, and MTwiNS. My work typically involves Epigenome-Wide Association Studies (EWAS) and analyses of various outcomes related to cognition and health over the life-course, with a particular focus on the biomolecular mechanisms leading to health disparities in Americans.
